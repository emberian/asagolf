Mechanized, not asserted

The substrate is a ZFC theorem

The of-* / √ axioms are the minimal ordered-Euclidean-field substrate the geometry needs (no completeness, no Archimedean). Each is mechanically bound to the metamath/set.mm theorem proving the same fact from ZFC (ax-sqrt ↔ resqrtth, of-recip ↔ axrrecex, …) — so they are modelled by the standard construction, shown by machine. Fully ZFC-grounded against set.mm the substrate is ≈1046.10 — but that is a routing artifact, and the descent proves it: native ZF model ≈1017.484, then (Infinity incidental; arithmetic and order discharged to verified proof) a fully-projection-free floor ≈1012.81. The proof-theoretic strength is an open theory below EFA; the irreducible residue is exactly two order facts. The one large number was always a model-choice artifact, never the geometry — and now it is measured all the way down.

What this means

The question

A poll estimated that the fully-expanded, machine-checked proof of a basic triangle-congruence theorem, reduced all the way to ZFC set theory, would be more than 101000 steps. Rather than argue with a Fermi estimate, this project builds the actual proof object: a small trusted Metamath-subset checker (the kernel), a hand-and-machine-assembled proof from ordered-ring axioms upward, and an exact cut-free step count that the kernel re-verifies. The answer is not close to 101000 — the geometry is around 105.58, kernel-verified and now closed end to end. But the rebuttal turned out to be the least interesting thing here. Chasing the cost all the way to the bottom showed that almost everything below the geometry was removable scaffolding we had chosen, not anything the mathematics required.

Derived, not asserted

The trivial route is to assert the Birkhoff postulates (ruler, protractor, SAS, …) as axioms; then "ASA" is about 200 steps and the question is vacuous. Here the postulates are instead derived. The substrate asserts only an ordered commutative ring with 1, one extra primitive (square roots of non-negatives exist — a Euclidean field), and ordinary logic with equality. Points, lines, distance, dot product and angle congruence are conservative definitions over coordinates, never axioms. Every Birkhoff postulate used is then a kernel-verified theorem proved from that substrate.

Why there is no blow-up in the geometry

Euclidean-plane geometry, expressed through these definitions, is just polynomial identities and order facts over the substrate. SAS — the hardest postulate — was about 7.25 million primitive steps fully expanded; proving the law of cosines once over fresh symbols and instantiating it for free (the generic-lemma template) cut it to 383,606, about 105.58, an astronomical distance below 101000. The geometry is cheap, and ASA′ is closed end to end with no postulate asserted.

The descent: every floor was a construction choice

The one genuinely large term is exhibiting a model of the substrate in ZFC. Against set.mm's analytically-constructed real field it is ≈1046.10 — but each time we got close enough to measure a floor, it turned out to be a thing we had chosen. It was not the real numbers; it was set.mm routing through analysis. Build a native ZF model and the floor drops to ≈1017.484; its residual looked like the Axiom of Infinity, but the proof never iterates it — the floor drops again to ≈1012.81, and the substrate is then fully projection-free. ℝ, completeness, Infinity, ω, ℚ, even arithmetic — all stripped, measured, shown removable.

What would not move

One thing did not dissolve. The inference a²=b² ⇒ a=b is false in ordinary rings (3²≡1² in ℤ/8, 3≠1); no polynomial identity of any degree crosses it — only order does. Three independent measurements — what the proof says, how strong it is, what its model is built from — all terminate on the same irreducible residue: exactly two order facts, ∅∈suc∅ and suc∅≠∅ — there is a next thing, and it is not the first. That is the whole intrinsic cost of metric geometry; everything else, chased to the bottom and measured, was scaffolding. See the cost-structure ledger and the novelization.

The same theorem is valid in the real reals

The grounded proof never uses any property beyond the substrate axioms — no completeness, no Archimedean property, no particular construction. So it holds in every Euclidean field: the real numbers, the real algebraic numbers, real-closed fields, the computable reals, and so on. Logically, the substrate axioms are all theorems of the real field, so the proof transfers upward to the standard real numbers unchanged; the minimal algebraic model is enough to establish validity, and the expensive real construction inherits exactly the same theorem. The cheap proof is the portable one — a proof that leaned on completeness would not transfer down.

Reading the sizes

Two numbers matter. The inlined size is the cut-free count with no sub-proof reuse at all — a deliberately pessimistic metric. The shared size is the same proof kept as a directed acyclic graph with common sub-proofs shared once; it is far smaller and is what a real proof library stores. Both are exact and re-checked by the kernel. For the full argument and the per-step ledger, see the deep dive.
