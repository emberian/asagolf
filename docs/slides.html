The poll claim

"Fully expand the proof of ASA (angle-side-angle triangle congruence) down to ZFC / first-order logic. It's more than 101000 primitive steps."

Two ways to answer:

  • Model it. Hand-wave a branching factor and a depth. Unfalsifiable.
  • Build it. A trusted kernel, a real proof object, an exact count it re-verifies. ← this

The trust root: a micro-kernel

  • A small Metamath-subset verifier (src/kernel.rs), 4 self-tests. That's the entire trusted base.
  • Substrate axioms: an ordered commutative ring with 1, one square-root primitive (Euclidean field), and ordinary logic with equality.
  • Points, lines, distance, dot product, angle congruence are conservative definitions over coordinates — never axioms.

Metric: every $a primitive invocation counts 1; definitions and lemmas expand. Cut-free, deliberately pessimistic.

Derived, not asserted

The trick that makes the question non-vacuous:

Approach"ASA" costHonest?
Assert Birkhoff postulates (ruler, protractor, SAS…) as axioms ~200 stepsNo — vacuous
Derive every postulate from the ring + √ substrate measured hereYes

Each Birkhoff postulate used is a kernel-verified theorem, proved bottom-up from ax-1/2/3/mp and the ordered-ring axioms.

The wall — and the breakthrough

The naive route fed the ring normaliser a dense degree-8 coordinate identity. It ran for over ten minutes and never finished.

  • Fix 1 — O(n⁴) → O(n³) normaliser: thread suffix endpoints instead of rebuilding the term list per swap.
  • Fix 2 — generic-Plücker factoring: prove tiny identities once over fresh atoms (a 2×2 Plücker relation, sign/EQ splits), then instantiate the big subterms by substitution — zero per-instance normalisation.

Same idea reused for every high-degree postulate.

Where the large number actually lives

Not in the geometry. In constructing ℝ.

Layerlog₁₀ steps
Geometry (SAS, kernel-exact)6.9
Minimal Euclidean-field model (√ a primitive) ~37
Full ZFC model of ℝ (set.mm) — dominated by analytic √ (resqrtth) ~45.7

Completeness (Dedekind/Cauchy) is never used by the geometry — and is a lower order than √ anyway. The cost is the construction of the field, not any deep theorem about it.

101000 is off by ~950 orders

Linear in log₁₀, 100 → 101000. Geometry and the entire ZFC real construction sit hard against the left edge. The poll's estimate is the lone marker on the right.

Takeaway

Euclidean geometry, derived honestly to a ring + √ substrate, is cheap — about 106.9 steps for the hardest postulate.

The only astronomical cost is building ℝ inside ZFC (~1045.7) — still ~950 orders below the poll's 101000.
