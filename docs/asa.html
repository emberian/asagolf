How big is a "simple" proof, really?

A poll guessed more than ten to the thousandth. So we built the proof object and let a sound kernel count it. The geometry came back at ten to the five-point-six — and the interesting result was not the size at all.

asagolf · every number kernel-verified, extracted, or labelled a projection · sources: README · HISTORY · NARRATIVE · COST_STRUCTURE · SUBSTRATE_FLOOR · SDG_MODEL

I The question, and the method

It began with a number someone said out loud without building it. A Twitter poll estimated that the fully-expanded, machine-checked proof of a basic triangle-congruence theorem — ASA, in a Birkhoff ruler/protractor formalism, reduced all the way down to ZFC — exceeds 101000 steps. Nobody had pushed it. An unbuilt number is not a measurement; it is a rumor about one.

So the method is the answer: build the proof object and count it. A small sound Metamath-subset verifier — src/kernel.rs, the entire trust story — re-checks hand-built proofs and reports exact cut-free step counts. The headline counts are deliberately cut-free, fully-inlined, with no lemma reuse: that is the metric, because it makes "size of a fully-expanded proof" concrete. If verify() returns Ok, every $p was derived from the $a axioms by substitution and the stack discipline. Nothing else has to be believed.

There is one law, older than any of the code, and this page is bound by it too:

Reported, not faked. Every number measured or extracted; anything else labelled a projection, with its derivation shown, never merged into a measured line.

A project that exists to rebut an over-claim cannot itself over-claim. So every figure below carries its exact provenance tag as it appears in the in-repo artifacts: measured by the kernel, extracted from the set.mm proof DAG, or a labelled projection with its derivation. Three quantities are reported and never summed — conflating them is the exact cheat this project exists to avoid.

II F0 = 224 — the cheap, vacuous answer

If you simply assert the seven Birkhoff postulates as axioms and prove ASA directly on top of them, the fully-expanded cut-free proof is exactly 224 steps measured (≈ 102.4; cargo run --bin asa, source: README results table). That is the entire honest content of the "> 101000" objection, answered in three digits.

It is also vacuous. Asserting the postulates assumes precisely the geometric facts that make the theorem hard; nothing has been grounded. F0 = 224 is the number you get for free, and it is reported here only so the honest number has something to be measured against. Everything after this is the non-vacuous version: every Birkhoff postulate derived, none asserted.

III "No cheating," defined precisely

The substrate F1 is an ordered commutative ring with 1, exactly one square-root primitive (ax-sqrt, principal root), and first-order logic with equality. Nothing geometric is an axiom. Points, lines, distance, dot product, angle congruence, the oriented-area discriminator (crs) and the ruler point (cp) are conservative definitions (df-*), eliminable, scored Definition — never GenuineAxiom.

Every Birkhoff postulate — ruler G1, incidence G2, ray-angle G3a, protractor-uniqueness G3b, ray-line G3c, SAS G4, congruence-substitution G0 — is a kernel-verified $p proved bottom-up from F1, none asserted as $a. The end-to-end re-expression, ASA′, is a complete no-cheating closure: asaprime prints COMPLETE NO-CHEATING CLOSURE with no PENDING $a and no open leaf. All seven postulates are kernel-verified in one run — 96 lemmas, a 267-statement database, verified all ✔ measured (source: README, honest status).

IV The generic-lemma golf

The size weapon is the generic-lemma template: prove a tiny identity once over fresh $f atoms, then instantiate big subterms by substitution — which is free in a cut-free metric. The headline counts stay cut-free and fully-inlined; the golf only changes which lemma is proved generically, never what is counted.

  • 3.18M → 67kloclink (the law-of-cosines link) — 3,180,000 → 67,217 measured
  • 390k → 68ksqcong — 390,380 → 68,276 measured
  • ~19×G4 SAS 7,251,714 → 383,606; 106.86 → 105.58, still verified all 96 ✔

A kernel-gated metasearcher closes the loop honestly. S1 (normalizer-strategy search) returns a kernel-verified negative: the production normalizer is already optimal-spine, so association/order is not a remaining lever. S2 plus the CSE minimizer give a sound, kernel-re-verified −81% on the shared-DAG total (10,134,529 → 1,916,586 measured) — but provably cannot move the cut-free figure, because CSE is invisible to a fully-inlined metric by construction. That wall is not an apology: it is exactly why cut-free is the honest hard metric for the poll's question.

V The substrate descent — every floor a construction choice

The question changed under the work. It stopped being how big and became where does the bigness live. The geometry is small; the cost is in grounding F1 in ZFC. Three quantities, kept rigorously apart and never summed (source: COST_STRUCTURE §1):

The three quantities · never summed

1 · Geometry over F1 — G4 SAS = 383,606 cut-free (105.58), ASA′ closed no-cheating end-to-end. measured

2 · Grounding cost — expand F1 → ZFC via set.mm's library: 1045.74 analytic-ℝ; 1030.75 via a real-closed-field route (no completeness); a strict extractable floor of 1025.95. extracted

3 · Satisfiability — exhibit a model of F1. This is where the real structure was hiding.

The grounding split is exact, not asserted. set.mm's 1045.74 is not completeness and not the completion definitions (0.6%): it is ℤ→ℚ pair-plumbing over any constructed ℝ. The achievable real-closed-field floor is 1030.75 extracted (dominated by the constructed twin axmulass), with a strict extractable lower bound of 1025.95 extracted (cheapest needed twin, axresscn); completeness ax-pre-sup is genuinely excluded and is lower-order anyway (1030.85). A minimal real-closed field is not mechanized — set.mm contains no such construction, so that number is not invented (source: SUBSTRATE_FLOOR, honest residual).

Satisfiability — where the structure was hiding

F1 is consistent and non-vacuous: a minimal model is constructed and kernel-measured. The generic √-extension step is 141 leaves (102.149). The closed ASA′ proof is measured to force exactly one un-nested radical (K = 1), so the proof-relativized construction is a fully measured 102.149 measured — no projection. (A model of the entire F1 schema stays a separate, explicitly labelled projection.) This proves F1 does not need ℝ.

Certifying that model through set.mm's existing library still costs ≈ 1046 extracted — everything there routes through analytic ℝ, and set.mm has no cheaper √-of-nonneg to certify against. That refusal to fake a smaller number sharpens the thesis. Routing F1 through a native ZF model instead — ℚ built from ω, plus one quadratic extension, with √-satisfaction a kernel-proved theorem — collapses the certified substrate to 1017.484 extracted, machine-checked: a ~1028.6-order collapse. The residual seemed to be the Axiom of Infinity — but Seam #1 then measured even Infinity incidental (the obligation is quantifier-free over finitely many named terms; suc applied once), and Stage 3 realized the finite ℚ-elements as hereditarily-finite sets, discharging the last inductive projection into a finite kernel check. Frontier B then discharged all arithmetic to kernel-$p from bare ZF.

  • 1046.10set.mm √-routing (CCfld/analytic √) extracted
  • 1017.484native ZF model, √ a kernel theorem — set.mm's analytic-√ routing removed extracted
  • 1012.810HF carrier (Vω), via sucexg — the ω-tower (Axiom of Infinity) removed; projection-free, fully-measured for all arithmetic extracted

Each level that looked like bedrock was a trapdoor. After every arithmetic fact is mechanically discharged, the entire residual is exactly two order literals: Q0 ≤ Q1 and Q1 ≠ Q0 on concrete ℚ-constants — decidable, non-inductive, Infinity-free. Closure 2 then discharged even those two literals to kernel-$p, and the discharge bottoms out, under an adversarial no-smuggle audit, at exactly two named bare-ZF primitives.

VI The convergence

Three independent attacks — what the proof says, how strong the proof is, what the model is built from — walked in from three different doors and stood on the same residue. This was measured, not arranged.

The convergence · three threads triangulate

Frontier C (proof content): the order predicate is proven not expressible as any ring identity — a theorem, localized to of-letot.

Frontier A (proof strength): the closed proof is exactly an open quantifier-free theory below EFA — 0 quantifiers, 0 induction, ≠ RCF, far below RCA₀. measured + bounded; made exact by Closure 1 (the df-* layer machine-certified conservative).

Frontier B (substrate construction): strip ℝ, Infinity, ω, ℚ and every arithmetic computation, and what is left, irreducibly, is two order inequalities on rational constants. measured

Proof-content, proof-strength and set-construction — entirely independent — all triangulate on order / orientation, and nothing else. Every other floor was removable scaffolding. The irreducible residue of Euclidean metric geometry is exactly two bare-ZF facts:

{ ∅ ∈ suc ∅ ,   suc ∅ ≠ ∅ }
there is a next thing, and it is not the first.

These are el-0-suc (the von-Neumann reflection of the order axiom of-letot) and ne-suc-0 (its Foundation companion) — precisely what Frontier C proved is not algebra. Discharging order is possible, but it re-incurs precisely of-letot's bare-ZF reflection: the loop closes on itself.

VII The one floor that would not move

Every floor chased dissolved under a better construction — except one. The load-bearing step inside sqcong says something a child knows: if two things have the same square and point the same way, they are the same thing. A subagent, briefed to be merciless, did the small brutal arithmetic.

In ℤ/8, 3² ≡ 1² ≡ 1, and 3 ≠ 1. The inference is false in an ordinary commutative ring. No polynomial identity of any degree gets you across it. Only order does.

Frontier C makes this a theorem, not an appearance. Ring identities are sound over every commutative ring; sqcong's conclusion u²=v²→u=v is false in ℤ/8, and its load-bearing internal step x²=0→x=0 is false in ℤ/4 (2² ≡ 0, 2 ≠ 0). By contraposition, no set of ring identities of any degree can express the order-core; it is logically essential, localized to the one named axiom of-letot ("no nonzero nilpotents"). This is the first and only intrinsic floor in the entire project — order is provably not algebra.

Partitioning every cut-free leaf (kernel-reverified, 9,017,010 $a measured): SYNTAX is 94.76% — writing the coordinate formulas down, a separate bucket never folded into "difficulty." Of proof content: loclink (law of cosines) reduces 96.82% to generic substitution-instances — near-perfect — but sqcong reduces only 38.48%; the other 61.52% is irreducible ordered-field sign reasoning that is provably not a polynomial identity.

The one open question · named, not papered

What remains genuinely open is only the quantitative question: Obligation O — an unconditional cut-free lower bound for x²=0 → x=0 — i.e. that the 61.52% sign-reasoning constant is minimal. The factor-through clause is provable; the size clause is open. It is named here, not hidden. A page that overclaims would be the cardinal public sin for a project that exists to rebut overclaiming. Companion open datum: tree-vs-DAG ratio is a small constant in [0.59×, 3.35×] for a fixed instance — explicitly not a Frege/extended-Frege separation theorem; the minimal-DAG lower bound is open (smallest-grammar is NP-hard).

VIII The dual — cheap proofs, immovable meaning

There was a hinge at the bottom of Book One, visible only from the floor. The thing that would not move — x²=0 ⟹ x=0 — is the exact axiom that synthetic differential geometry is built by refusing. Keep the nilpotents it forbids and the derivative falls out of an axiom instead of a limit. Book Two opens that door with the same witness, which does not care which logic you hand it.

Book Two has its own dual law: where Book One's cardinal sin was asserting what should be proven, Book Two's is assuming what should be refused — a classical principle smuggled into an intuitionistic proof. The witness has read it: verified all 8 $p in data/sdg.mm ✔ measured. The purity guard was given teeth and rejects excluded middle, double-negation, Peirce and decidable equality even under innocent names; all forty logical axioms came back pure (source: NARRATIVE, Book Two; SDG_MODEL).

Book Two keeps its own dual pair of quantities, never summed. The synthetic proofs are measured: the seam-free first synthetic derivative (sdg-deriv) is 2,243 leaves; the threaded sum rule (sdg-global-sum) is 23,508 leaves. The model grounding is, by design and on principle, a labelled projection throughout — there is no Metamath/ZFC formalization of a well-adapted topos in any library we possess, so no model-cost number is invented; the honest analog computes only a structural citation-ladder depth.

The recurring theorem across both books: cheap pure content, immovable meaning-cost. The first synthetic derivative exists by Kock–Lawvere and is unique by microcancellation, with nothing classical spent to say so. One seam is open and named: the universal linking the pointwise law to the global one is handed in rather than threaded. The mirror hypothesis is not proven — it is supported, which is the honest word, and the only one the law allows.
