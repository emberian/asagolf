step 1 — make it concrete

A proof is a finite object.
Re-check it with something tiny.

  • A proof is a finite tree of axiom-instances and substitutions — you can print it and machine-check it.
  • One small, sound Metamath-subset kernel (src/kernel.rs) re-verifies every step. It is the only trusted code.
  • Everything else — the proof search, the normaliser, all of it — is untrusted. If the kernel says Ok, it's a proof. That's the LCF / de Bruijn discipline.
[1:00–2:30] "First move: make the question concrete. You know this from proof theory — a proof is a finite syntactic object, a tree whose leaves are axiom instances and whose nodes are inference steps. Finite means countable. So we wrote a tiny verifier — a sound subset of Metamath, a few hundred lines, four self-tests — and that kernel is the ONLY thing we trust. Every proof we build, however it was found, gets handed back to that kernel and re-checked from the axioms by substitution alone. This is exactly the LCF idea, de Bruijn's criterion: a small trusted core, an arbitrarily clever untrusted mantle. When I say a number is 'verified' in this talk, I mean that kernel returned Ok on that exact proof object." 90s.

step 2 — pick an honest metric

"Fully expanded" = cut-free, zero reuse

Expand every lemma. Inline every shared sub-proof. Reuse nothing. Count primitive axiom-instances.

  • This is the most pessimistic honest size — we deliberately picked the metric that's biggest.
  • You know the catch from cut-elimination: a one-line statement can have a cut-free proof that is non-elementarily larger than the proof with lemmas.
  • So the question isn't loaded in our favour. If anything blows up, this metric finds it.

Convention: each $a primitive instance counts 1; every definition and lemma is expanded away. (We also report the DAG-shared size; it's far smaller — that's the point of cut.)

[2:30–4:00] "Second move: choose the metric, and choose it AGAINST ourselves. 'Fully expanded' means cut-free: expand every lemma, inline every reused sub-proof, reuse nothing, count primitive axiom instances. You all know why that's the dangerous metric — cut elimination. A short theorem can have a normal-form proof that's hyper-exponentially bigger than the one with lemmas; Statman, Orevkov, the standard blow-up results. So by picking the cut-free count we are NOT making it easy on ourselves — we're deliberately using the metric most likely to vindicate a 10^1000 claim. Foreshadow: keep that blow-up in your pocket; it shows up live in a few slides." 90s. Land 'cut-free = pessimistic on purpose'.

step 3 — don't cheat

The trap: assert the postulates ⇒ the question is vacuous

If you…"ASA" costsverdict
assert Birkhoff's ruler / protractor / SAS as axioms 224 steps vacuous — you assumed the hard parts
derive every postulate from a minimal substrate the real question honest

Birkhoff "tidiness" is a layer illusion. It doesn't remove the cost — it relocates it into one phrase: "…now construct ℝ so that distance and angle are real-valued."

So: derive ASA from postulates we prove, and ground the substrate honestly. F0 ASA = 224 is exact and kernel-verified — and it's exactly the move we are refusing to call an answer.

[4:00–5:30] "Third move, and it's the conceptual hinge. There's a cheap answer: take Birkhoff's axioms — the ruler postulate, the protractor postulate, SAS — as axioms. Then ASA is a 224-step proof. We verified that; it's exact. And it's vacuous, because Birkhoff's elegance is a LAYER illusion. Axiomatising the ruler and protractor doesn't make the cost go away — it relocates ALL of it into one sentence the textbook waves past: 'let distance and angle be real numbers.' Constructing those reals is where the bodies are buried. So to answer the poll honestly we do two things: derive every Birkhoff postulate as a theorem from a minimal substrate, and then ground that substrate. No assuming the hard parts." 90s.

step 5 — doing it · the technical heart

We hit the cut-free wall — live, in our own engine

The geometry is just polynomial identities + order facts. The naive normaliser was handed one tiny statement: a dense degree-8 identity in 8 variables.

  • It ran >10 minutes and never finished. That's the thesis of this talk, happening to us: a tiny statement, an astronomical cut-free proof.
  • The fix is mathematical, not engineering. Prove a small generic identity once over fresh variables; then instantiate by substitution. Substitution doesn't re-expand.
  • One idea — and it cascaded to all 7 postulates.
[7:00–10:00] THE TECHNICAL HEART — slow down here, 3 full minutes. "Now we actually do it. Good news: through those coordinate definitions, all of Euclidean geometry is just polynomial identities and order inequalities over the field. So we built a normaliser and started proving postulates. Then the angle postulate handed the normaliser one statement — a single dense degree-8 polynomial identity in eight coordinate variables. The naive expansion ran for over ten minutes and never finished. Pause on that. We had just, accidentally, REPRODUCED the poll's claim inside our own engine: a one-line statement whose fully-expanded cut-free proof is astronomical. That is exactly cut-elimination blow-up, live. [click] The temptation is to optimise the normaliser. We did profile it and fix a quadratic, but the real lesson is the honest one: a dense degree-8 cut-free proof is INTRINSICALLY astronomical — that's our own thesis, not a bug. The fix had to be mathematical. Prove the needed identity ONCE, generically, over fresh abstract variables — a small proof — then instantiate it with the giant coordinate subterms by SUBSTITUTION. Exact machine attribution of the 1045.7: it is not completeness, and not even the completion definitions (0.6%). It's ℤ→ℚ pair-plumbing multiplicity — a function of which ℝ you build, never of the geometry.

[10:00–12:30] "So where did the poll's 10^1000 go? Look at the spectrum. The geometry is the green sliver, 10^6.9, fixed in every model. The ONLY astronomical term is constructing the reals inside ZFC: 10^45.7. And here's the result I most want you to take away — we did an exact, occurrence-level machine attribution of that number. It is NOT completeness — Dedekind/Cauchy completeness is never invoked by the geometry and is lower order anyway. It's not even the completion DEFINITIONS — those are zero-point-six percent. It is integer-to-rational pair-plumbing multiplicity: the bookkeeping cost every field operation pays over a fully constructed real number. Which means the big number is a function of WHICH real numbers you build. Set.mm's maximalist construction: 45.7. Take square root as a Euclidean-field primitive instead: 37.2. Drop completion entirely — real-closed fields, which by Tarski are decidable with a quantifier- elimination decision procedure and consistency strength near PRA, far below ZFC: lower still. The geometry never moves. The cost is the construction, and you chose the construction." 150s.

the soul of the project

The best results were the refusals

This rebuts an over-claim — so it must not over-claim.

  • We proved a postulate false. Bare protractor-uniqueness is provably false in the √-free squared-cosine encoding (mirror-blind; explicit counterexample) — so we proved the faithful oriented form instead.
  • The assembler refuses to lie. The end-to-end re-expression (asaprime) currently stops dead and names the exact missing wiring — Birkhoff's SAS is angle-output, our verified G4-sas is side-output — rather than print a green checkmark it hasn't earned.
  • Stated plainly: all 7 postulates verified, no cheating; the literal end-to-end ASA′ is the last finite step and is honestly in progress.
[13:30–15:00] DO NOT SKIP. This is the soul. "Foundations people should be suspicious of a talk that rebuts an over-claim, because the rebuttal can over-claim too. So let me be exact about what we did NOT prove. Two things, and I'm proud of both. One: we tried to prove protractor-uniqueness and found the natural statement is provably FALSE in our square-root-free squared-cosine encoding — it's mirror-blind, and we have an explicit counterexample. So we proved the faithful ORIENTED version instead. Two: the program that wires the postulates into one end-to-end ASA proof currently STOPS and tells you precisely what's missing — our verified SAS outputs a side, Birkhoff's outputs an angle, and the angle-output law-of-cosines lemma isn't among the 87 yet — rather than print a checkmark it hasn't earned. All seven postulates are kernel-verified with no cheating; the literal final assembly is one named finite step, in progress, and we say so. The foundations punchline: 'cut-free size' turns an abstract notion concrete; 'tidiness' is relative to your foundation; the cost lives in the construction, not the theorem; and every digit is, or will be, kernel-checked." 90s.
